# Domain

**Domain:** Knowledge representation and engineering

---

## Conceptual Definition

A domain is a bounded region of discourse within which terms, concepts, and relationships have their intended, fixed meanings, and outside of which those meanings do not apply or may differ.

**Necessary conditions:**
1. **Bounded** — it has explicit or implicit scope boundaries that separate it from other regions of discourse.
2. **Determinate** — within its boundaries, every term has exactly one intended interpretation.
3. **Coherent** — the concepts and relationships within it are mutually consistent.

**Sufficient conditions:**  
Any region of discourse that is bounded, determinate in its interpretation of terms, and internally coherent constitutes a domain.

---

## Operational Definition

A domain is operationally identified when:

1. A set of terms can be listed whose meanings are fixed and agreed within a specific community or system.
2. A boundary condition can be stated: a term used outside this boundary may have a different or undefined meaning.
3. An ontology, schema, or controlled vocabulary exists (or can be constructed) that governs interpretation within the boundary.

---

## Distinguished From

| Term | Distinction |
|------|-------------|
| **Subject area** | An informal clustering of topics; a domain additionally requires fixed, formal term interpretations. |
| **Namespace** | A namespace provides syntactic scoping of identifiers; a domain provides semantic scoping of meanings. |
| **Ontology** | An ontology is a formal specification *within* a domain; the domain is the region of discourse the ontology describes. |
