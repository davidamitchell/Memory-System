# Fact

**Domain:** Knowledge representation and engineering

---

## Conceptual Definition

A fact is a proposition that has been verified as true within a defined domain and knowledge base, and that is recorded as a confirmed, non-provisional assertion about the state of that domain.

**Necessary conditions:**
1. **Propositional** — it is a statement with a defined subject and predicate that has a truth value.
2. **True** — it accurately corresponds to the state of affairs in the domain.
3. **Verified** — its truth has been established through a defined process (observation, inference from axioms, source attestation).
4. **Recorded** — it is explicitly stored or derivable within a knowledge base.
5. **Domain-bounded** — its scope is fixed by the domain in which verification was performed.

**Sufficient conditions:**  
Any proposition that is true, verified, and recorded within a knowledge base for a defined domain constitutes a fact.

---

## Operational Definition

A fact is operationally identified when:

1. It is expressed as a triple or assertion that is present in the knowledge base (asserted or derivable by the reasoner).
2. Its provenance is traceable: the source and verification method are recorded.
3. No contradicting verified assertion exists within the same knowledge base.
4. It can be used as a premise in valid inferences to derive additional facts.

---

## Distinguished From

| Term | Distinction |
|------|-------------|
| **Information** | Information is contextualised data that is interpretable but not necessarily verified; a fact is verified as true. |
| **Insight** | An insight is a derived, non-obvious statement obtained by reasoning over facts; a fact is a directly verified base-level assertion. |
| **Assertion** | An assertion is any recorded statement, verified or not; a fact is specifically a verified true assertion. |
| **True** | True is the property; fact is the entity (a proposition) that possesses that property and has been verified. |
