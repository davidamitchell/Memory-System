# Document

**Domain:** Knowledge representation and engineering

---

## Conceptual Definition

A document is a discrete, self-contained, addressable unit of recorded content that has defined boundaries, a unique identity, and carries one or more statements about one or more subjects.

**Necessary conditions:**
1. **Discrete** — it exists as a bounded artifact distinguishable from all other documents.
2. **Self-contained** — its content is cohesive and interpretable without requiring merger with another document.
3. **Addressable** — it can be uniquely identified and located (e.g., by a URI, filename, or identifier).
4. **Recorded** — its content is fixed at a point in time and persists independently of its author's presence.
5. **Carries statements** — it asserts or records one or more propositions about subjects.

**Sufficient conditions:**  
Any discrete, self-contained, addressable, recorded artifact that carries statements constitutes a document.

---

## Operational Definition

A document is operationally identified when:

1. It has a unique identifier (path, URI, slug).
2. Its content can be retrieved and read independently of other documents.
3. It has metadata describing its identity, provenance, and subject matter.
4. It can be added to or removed from a corpus as a unit without partial inclusion.

---

## Distinguished From

| Term | Distinction |
|------|-------------|
| **Record** | A record is typically a structured data entry within a larger store; a document is independently interpretable content with its own identity. |
| **Resource** | A resource is any identifiable entity (including documents); a document is specifically a recorded-content resource. |
| **Individual** | An individual is an instance in an ontology; a document is the physical/digital artifact that may describe or constitute evidence for individuals. |
