# Insight

**Domain:** Knowledge representation and engineering

---

## Conceptual Definition

An insight is a derived statement that reveals a non-obvious relationship, pattern, or implication, obtained by inference or analysis over a set of facts, that was not directly observable in the source material and that has explanatory or actionable value.

**Necessary conditions:**
1. **Derived** — it is the output of an inferential or analytical process over facts; it is not directly observed or asserted.
2. **Non-obvious** — it is not entailed by a single fact in isolation; its value lies in the connection it draws across multiple facts or abstractions.
3. **Grounded** — it is traceable to specific facts or evidence; it is not speculative beyond what the evidence supports.
4. **Explanatory or actionable** — it contributes to understanding why or how something is the case, or it enables a decision or action that could not be made without it.

**Sufficient conditions:**  
Any derived statement that is grounded in facts, reveals a non-obvious relationship or pattern, and provides explanatory or actionable value constitutes an insight.

---

## Operational Definition

An insight is operationally identified when:

1. Its derivation can be traced to specific source facts and an explicit inference or analysis method.
2. It could not be found by retrieving any single source fact or document.
3. It changes or refines the state of the knowledge base: a new class relationship, a gap in coverage, an unexpected dependency.
4. An agent acting on it would behave differently than an agent without it.

---

## Distinguished From

| Term | Distinction |
|------|-------------|
| **Fact** | A fact is a directly verified base assertion; an insight is derived and reveals something beyond the directly observed facts. |
| **Inference** | An inference is any conclusion derivable by a formal rule; an insight is an inference that is non-obvious and has significant explanatory or actionable value. Not all inferences are insights. |
| **Hypothesis** | A hypothesis is an unverified conjecture; an insight is grounded in verified facts. |
