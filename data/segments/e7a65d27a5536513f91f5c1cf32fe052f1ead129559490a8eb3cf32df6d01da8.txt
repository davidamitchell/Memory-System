Claims:
- [fact] Lean Startup treats validated learning, innovation accounting, milestones, and the build-measure-learn loop as the management system that decides whether to pivot, persevere, and widen commitment. Source: Eric Ries, "Methodology."
- [fact] O'Reilly and Tushman argue that ambidextrous organisations separate exploratory and exploitative work structurally but use targeted integration and senior-team alignment to connect resources and capabilities. Source: O'Reilly and Tushman 2013 working paper.
- [fact] Thoughtworks says production-ready AI requires foundational architecture, operating model alignment, trustworthy controls, and governed data rather than model experimentation alone. Source: Thoughtworks whitepaper.
- [fact] CIO highlights practical enablers such as AI centres of excellence, enablement squads, pre-approved tools and methods, and guardrails that let more teams adopt capability without each team reinventing the control stack. Source: Chief Information Officer (CIO) article.
- [fact] McKinsey links higher value capture to management practices across operating model, adoption and scaling, and technology rather than to technical experimentation alone. Source: McKinsey 2025.
- [inference] The strongest synthesis-preserving pattern is to time-box exploration, then require a minimum viable synthesis pack before broader exploitation. That pack should capture the tested use case, failure conditions, ownership, guardrails, operating pattern, training needs, metrics, and explicit scale-hold-stop decision. Basis: Lean Startup, Thoughtworks, McKinsey, and Chief Information Officer (CIO).
- [inference] Shared enablement mechanisms and senior-team integration reduce synthesis cost by centralising reusable decisions once, then distributing them across many exploitation efforts. Basis: O'Reilly and Tushman, Chief Information Officer (CIO), Thoughtworks.
