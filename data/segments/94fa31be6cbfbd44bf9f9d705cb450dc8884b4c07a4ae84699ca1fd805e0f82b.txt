| Term | Distinction |
|------|-------------|
| **Knowledge** | Knowledge is the content (verified facts and their relationships); wisdom is the capacity to judge its appropriate application. |
| **Insight** | An insight is a derived true statement; wisdom is the meta-capacity to judge the relevance and application of insights and knowledge. |
| **Intelligence** | Intelligence is the general capacity to learn and reason; wisdom specifically concerns evaluative, contextualised judgment in the application of knowledge. |
