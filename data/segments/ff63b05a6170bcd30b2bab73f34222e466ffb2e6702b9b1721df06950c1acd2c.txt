- [fact] Technical lens: the missing work before scale is not merely documentation; it includes architecture, data access, workflow integration, guardrails, and observability decisions that determine whether exploitation is actually repeatable. Source: Thoughtworks; McKinsey; California Management Review.
- [fact] Economic lens: pilot stagnation and low scale-up rates imply that exploration spend can fail to compound into value when the organisation does not convert local learning into shared capability. Source: Thoughtworks; Boston Consulting Group via prior portfolio item.
- [fact] Behavioural lens: synthesis remains structurally under-rewarded because novelty, local credit, and visible demos outperform codification in short-horizon incentive systems. Source: prior exploration-synthesis gap item.
- [fact] Historical lens: Lean Startup and ambidexterity research both show that experimentation without explicit integration decisions is not a new problem; fast-moving AI intensifies an older organisational tension rather than replacing it. Source: Eric Ries; O'Reilly and Tushman.
- [fact] Regulatory and governance lens: codified ownership, accountability, and standards matter because scale introduces cross-team risk that isolated experiments can hide. Source: California Management Review; Thoughtworks.
- [inference] The synthesis step is valuable partly because it collapses these lenses into one management checkpoint: it forces the technical, economic, behavioural, and governance implications of a successful exploration to be made explicit before broad exploitation begins.
