1. An agent correctly applies knowledge in situations where the applicable rules conflict, are incomplete, or have not been previously encountered.
2. The agent's decisions can be retrospectively justified by appeal to goals, values, and consequences — not merely to rules.
3. The agent knows when *not* to apply a piece of knowledge (recognising scope limits).
4. Outcomes produced by the agent are better (by agreed criteria) than those produced by rule-following alone.
