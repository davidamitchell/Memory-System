Claims:
- [fact] Thoughtworks argues that many Artificial Intelligence (AI) initiatives fail to scale not because the model fails, but because organisations reach scale with fragmented data, brittle architectures, disconnected workflows, and unresolved governance and ownership. Source: Thoughtworks whitepaper, opening problem statement.
- [fact] Thoughtworks defines readiness for scale across multiple dimensions, including foundational architecture, operating model, data readiness, and trustworthy AI controls. Source: Thoughtworks whitepaper, readiness dimensions.
- [fact] McKinsey reports that management practices across strategy, talent, operating model, technology, data, and adoption and scaling all correlate positively with value attributable to AI. Source: McKinsey, "The State of AI: Global Survey 2025," summary section on six dimensions.
- [fact] California Management Review states that effective scaling depends less on technical capability alone than on governance structures that clarify ownership, codify standards, and make accountability visible. Source: California Management Review, governance section.
- [fact] The prior exploration-synthesis gap item found that unsynthesised exploration creates knowledge-loss risk, especially when agent-driven work leaves no durable process record for other humans to reuse. Source: `Research/completed/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.md`, Executive Summary and Analysis.
- [inference] No reviewed source defines a universally named "synthesis step" as a canonical formal phase; the step is a cross-source construct inferred from repeated pre-scale requirements. Basis: reviewed sources above.
- [inference] The synthesis step is the codification and decision phase that converts experimental learning into an exploitation commitment by producing explicit artefacts: decision logs, validated use and failure conditions, operating model choices, ownership, standards, guardrails, reusable patterns, training material, and success metrics. Basis: Thoughtworks, McKinsey, California Management Review, and prior exploration-synthesis gap item.
