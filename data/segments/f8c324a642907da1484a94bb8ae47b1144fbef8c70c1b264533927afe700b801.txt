- [inference; source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12599-018-0542-4; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10257-022-00553-8; https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.06590; https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.09089; https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/announcing-the-2025-dora-report] Combining the automation-suitability evidence with the realistic AI-uplift range supports a bounded three-year answer: centrally governed software delivery is likely to absorb roughly one-quarter to one-half of current workaround automation demand, with results near the top of that range likely requiring a strong internal platform environment, an unusually high share of stable digital demand, and unusually low coordination debt.
