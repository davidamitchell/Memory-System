- **B2. When low-cost paths undermine relevance**
  - [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-26-measuring-opportunity-cost.html] Organisations systematically over-reward visible local cost savings and under-detect diffuse opportunity loss, which means a cheap local knowledge shortcut can look efficient even while degrading broader outcomes.
  - [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-08-productivity-incentive-metrics-quality-review-agentic-ai.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-02-hitl-review-volume-bottleneck-rubber-stamp.html] High-volume workflows can preserve visible throughput while degrading review quality, increasing hidden defects, and turning formal controls into nominal sign-off.
  - [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-26-measuring-opportunity-cost.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-08-productivity-incentive-metrics-quality-review-agentic-ai.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-02-hitl-review-volume-bottleneck-rubber-stamp.html] Low-effort knowledge paths become strategically harmful when they externalise evaluation work, quality risk, or opportunity loss onto another part of the organisation that does not control the pathway.
