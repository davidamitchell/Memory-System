- [fact] The prior exploration-synthesis gap item found four recurring forces that suppress synthesis: novelty reward, credit asymmetry, resistance to external knowledge, and knowledge stickiness. Source: `Research/completed/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.md`, Key Findings and Analysis.
- [fact] Thoughtworks describes a recurrent organisational tension in which business teams push for speed, information technology teams prioritise stability and control, and leaders demand visible value quickly. Source: Thoughtworks whitepaper, problem framing.

Basis: prior exploration-synthesis gap item plus Thoughtworks and McKinsey.
- [inference] Fast-moving technology amplifies the skip tendency because each new capability resets attention toward fresh exploration, while the unfinished codification work on the previous capability remains hidden. Basis: prior exploration-synthesis gap item and this item's context.
