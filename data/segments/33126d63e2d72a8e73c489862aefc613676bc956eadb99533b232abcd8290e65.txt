**Necessary conditions:**
1. **Linguistic** — it is a word or phrase in a natural or formal language.
2. **Domain-scoped** — its denotation is fixed within a specific domain; outside that domain it may denote differently or not at all.
3. **Univocal** — within its domain, it denotes exactly one concept (no polysemy within scope).
4. **Assigned** — the denotation is stipulated, not merely habitual; there is a definitional act binding term to concept.
