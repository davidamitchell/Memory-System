The synthesis step sits between them and is easy to skip under time pressure: it feels like overhead when the technology is already "proven" by the exploration and the business wants to move to exploitation immediately. The synthesis step is where the organisation:
1. Consolidates what was actually learned (not just "it works" but "here is when it works, when it fails, and at what cost")
2. Decides the exploitation pattern (how will this capability be used consistently, at scale, with what guardrails)
3. Produces the artefacts that make exploitation repeatable (runbooks, reference architectures, standards, training, policy)
4. Resets the portfolio signal (what new explorations are now warranted given what was learned)
