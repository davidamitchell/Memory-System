Source: Chief Information Officer (CIO) article, sections on centres of excellence, advisory squads, and guardrails.

- [inference] Skipping synthesis creates hidden adoption debt: teams re-explore solved questions, ship one-off implementations that cannot be governed consistently, lose learning when explorers move on, and realise less return on investment (ROI) from the same exploration spend. Basis: Thoughtworks, Chief Information Officer (CIO), Boston Consulting Group, and prior internal items.
