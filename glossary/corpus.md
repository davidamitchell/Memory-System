# Corpus

**Domain:** Knowledge representation and engineering

---

## Conceptual Definition

A corpus is a finite, purposively assembled collection of documents that serves as the primary source material for knowledge extraction, analysis, or ontology construction within a defined domain.

**Necessary conditions:**
1. **Finite** — the collection has defined membership; there is a fact of the matter about whether any given document is in or out.
2. **Purposive** — assembled with an explicit analytical or representational goal, not arbitrarily accumulated.
3. **Document-constituted** — composed exclusively of documents (discrete recorded-knowledge units).
4. **Domain-relevant** — all member documents pertain to the domain under study.

**Sufficient conditions:**  
Any finite, purposively assembled, domain-relevant collection of documents constitutes a corpus for that domain.

---

## Operational Definition

A corpus is operationally identified when:

1. Its membership criterion is stated (e.g., "all completed research items in the Research repository as of a given date").
2. Its purpose is declared (e.g., "source material for ontology construction").
3. Its boundaries can be tested: given any candidate document, it can be determined whether it is a member.
4. It can be iterated over exhaustively.

---

## Distinguished From

| Term | Distinction |
|------|-------------|
| **Dataset** | A dataset is a structured collection of data records; a corpus is a collection of documents (which may be unstructured or semi-structured). |
| **Archive** | An archive preserves documents for historical or compliance reasons without an explicit analytical purpose. |
| **Knowledge base** | A knowledge base contains formalised statements derived from a corpus; a corpus contains the source documents prior to formalisation. |
